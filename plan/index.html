Event Planning Guide

We are collecting and building resources to help you to organize your event

We're days away from posting a Content Guide for food-related events here, to give you some guidance on planning your event.
We'll also post the guide at the TeachOSM site (under development) so you can use it for future lesson plans, project ideas
or for your classroom, seminar, club.
  • Your can plan an outside surveying event, an inside editing event, or a mix of both. But please keep the theme related to food and food resources. Use National Geographic's Geography Awareness Week resources to generate ideas.
  • Add anything food-related to the map: groceries, restaurants, grain elevators, urban farms, community gardens, ...you name it.
  • Design your event friendly to newcomers. Make sure there's someone available to instruct on how to survey using Field Papers and how to edit OpenStreetMap using the Id editor.
  • Your event location should preferably have fast internet, comfortable seating, and work tables. If you're working at a school, bear in mind that their technologies might be constrained (older equipment, no admin privileges, etc.). In those cases, plan for work-arounds.
  • Changes committed to OpenStreetMap should contain the hashtag, #OSMGeoweek in the changeset comment.
  • Link to a post-event survey to gauge interest and ideas for improving future events.
